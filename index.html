Coding is one of the most magic things happened in my life. Starting from the word Hacker in scientific movie, a young girl thought she would become a strong-willed fighter towards unfair and wicked activities in the era of information. She believed technology should make our lives happier and more convenient, with beautiful software and novel electronic devices.

I entered the field of Electrical Engineering from the first day in college, stepping across the marble road with Morse code on it. And the affection for Computer Science began with the first C programing class. I found an analogue electronic keyboard could be made by welding electronic elements, and an electronic flute could be implemented by FPGA. At the meantime, I learnt to write small programs of Minesweeping and Painting software and felt great pleasure when they were completed.

I was soon attracted by Quadcopters when I saw the videos recorded by them. In a group of three, we used ultrasonic and barometer to access height control and used GPS module in positioning. I mainly contributed in Android remote control program to transmit position information through Bluetooth. During the process, I understood the hardship in debugging and testing products, as DJI Phantom has experienced before its success.

As a young girl pursuing fashion, I studied all kinds of photo and beatify software, and was curious about those makeup algorithms. I interned in Chinese Academic of Sciences, worked on a project about automatic makeup algorithm, and trained Generative Adversarial Networks with thousands of pictures from super stars. As is expected, AI showed its potential, and generated photos from random pixels to vivid portraits.

Now, I am in the project of software engineering course, which is Alpha Gomoku Game. We plan to build a desktop game joining Python and Java. The online version can provide separate room for player to invite their friends. In offline version, users can play with an AI player. It will also contain ranking board and background music to offer better user experience.
